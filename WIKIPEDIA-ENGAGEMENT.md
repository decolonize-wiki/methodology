# Wikipedia engagement policy

decolonize.wiki analyzes Wikipedia articles. Wikipedia is written by
volunteers, and its own community runs long-standing counter-systemic-bias
efforts. This policy keeps our relationship with that community honest.

1. **We critique articles, not editors.** No analysis names, blames, or
   speculates about the people who wrote a passage.
2. **We never direct or coordinate edits to Wikipedia.** Calls like "go fix
   this article" are off-wiki canvassing under Wikipedia policy; they get
   edits reverted and harm both projects. We publish analyses; what readers
   do with them is their own, individual choice.
3. **Contributors who edit Wikipedia disclose.** If you contribute here and
   also edit related Wikipedia articles, disclose your affiliation on your
   Wikipedia user page and follow the relevant WikiProject norms.
4. **We comply with CC BY-SA.** Every quote is attributed to its article and
   pinned revision, on pages and on share cards.
5. **When an article improves, we say it changed — not that we changed it.**
   Our banners read "this article has changed since analysis". We do not
   claim credit for edits.
6. **Disputes are public.** Every flag links to a public issue tracker where
   anyone — including Wikipedia editors — can challenge it.
