# Governance

Honest single-maintainer governance beats fake community governance. This
document says exactly how decisions are made today and how that changes as
the project grows.

## Maintainer

[@kaiomp](https://github.com/kaiomp) is the initial maintainer and merges all changes.

## Proposing a change

Open a PR (new category, edit to criteria, new sources). A proposal must:

1. **Cite its lineage.** Academic or non-academic sources carry equal
   standing (see README), but oral or non-academic sources must be attested:
   say who holds this knowledge and where it is recorded or witnessed.
2. **Pass the hostile-historian test.** Would a fair-minded historian with
   full context concede that the criterion identifies a real framing choice
   (not a disputed interpretation presented as the only reading)?
3. **Be checkable against text.** Criteria must be decidable by reading a
   passage — no criteria that require knowing an editor's intent.

## How contested PRs resolve

Discussion stays open at least 7 days. The maintainer decides and records
the reasoning in `DECISIONS.md`. Decisions can be revisited with new sources.

## Scope: what this methodology is not

PRs advancing colonial apologia (e.g. "colonization was net-positive"
framing) are out of scope and will be closed with a reference to this
section. This is a methodology for identifying colonial framing, not a forum
for relitigating whether colonialism was wrong.

## Path to maintainership

Sustained, high-quality contributions (accepted PRs, well-argued reviews)
lead to co-maintainership by invitation, recorded here.

## Versioning

Accepted changes to categories bump the methodology version (git tag).
Analyses record the version that produced them; version bumps mark older
analyses as superseded, they are never silently rewritten.
